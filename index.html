Centre Supports Times Feature

By Centre Supports Staff • January 2026    

Since its recent establishment, Centre Supports, a local community resource hub, has started a forward effort to build a website consolidating local businesses and nonprofits in Centre County PA, aiming to support the community. Starting 2026, the organization has implemented various new initiatives to improve user experience and expand outreach.

Through their many partners, the Centre Supports website provides insight on the missions and goals of various major businesses in the county. Through their business spotlights section, directors and staff alike can share their thoughts. “‘We support victims and survivors of sexual assault, dating, and domestic violence… we really are a safety net for people of all ages, identities, [and] backgrounds who are going through these things,” says Director of Centre Safe, Jennifer Pancek.

Regarding community support, Kelly Carpenter from Childspace has been working to improve the establishment for 2026. Childspace gives every kid a “...child-centered environment to be in…a lot of families don’t have the kinds of equipment and materials that we have here in a space where kids can be with peers and learn from peers more than they can when they’re at home.”

Action is being taken on a county-wide level as well; the Centre County Office of Aging has increased their number of programs: “Our goal is to keep elderly people at home as long as possible ...our services are based on supporting an individual of 60 years age or older; in most cases they surround trying to allow the older adult to maintain their living situation for as long as possible,” says Quentin Burchfield, Office of Aging director. Centre Supports aims to highlight these initiatives through their Youtube channel.

The site offers a user-friendly, efficient platform for community members, providing a wide range of resources and various accessibility features to improve user experience.

  • Hotlines for immediate help in emergencies
  • Reliable childcare services to support the local youth
  • Substance abuse and mental health resources
  • Knowledge on job opportunities and finances
  • Resources to improve access to housing and transportation

Each category offers different services that are tailored to help those struggling with various issues. It gives any Centre County individual access to local resources, shedding light on both small nonprofits and county-wide organizations. Centre Supports managers aim to create a cohesive, effective environment for the exploration of resources in Centre County. Additionally, the site includes a feedback page, allowing residents to request the inclusion of additional sites.

As for improvements around the county, interviewees have described great progress. Director Pancek describes, “We just launched our annual $50,000 holiday campaign to raise $50,000 to use for…our services and clients during the year” (Centre Safe). “I recently got a grant for $2,000.00 worth of outdoor equipment…we’re gonna be getting some new, more expensive equipment that I can’t usually afford on my regular budget… I just recently started offering drop-in services as well, so people who don’t need a consistent spot but might need something like a babysitter once in a while…have a place to bring their child that is convenient,” adds Ms. Carpenter. Learn more about progress and changes in Centre County in our seasonal newsletter!

As the local community continues to grow, Centre Supports looks to help anyone in need. They have high hopes for new partnerships, support connections, and fundraising opportunities in 2026.
